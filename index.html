How it works (concept)

The long-term structure: not just what a metric is, but how it is built.

01 · Select

Pick a stock or use a sample

In future iterations: search by ticker, or choose from a list of example companies. The engine pulls the core inputs (price, earnings, cash flow, equity, etc.).

02 · Compute

Calculate core metrics

For each stock, core metrics are computed: P/E, FCF yield, ROE, margins, growth rates, and more — always with the exact formula shown next to the value.

03 · Explain

Show the logic, not just the output

Every metric is accompanied by plain-language context: which inputs were used, how they were sourced, when the metric is useful, and when it becomes misleading.

Why this exists

The goal of Transparent Metrics is to build something different from a typical “type a ticker, get a number” website. Most platforms show P/E, ROE, or growth rates with no real transparency. This project is built around the opposite idea: the formulas and inputs are the core product, not an afterthought.

Long-term, the plan is to turn this into a calculator + explanation engine for investors who actually want to understand what they are looking at: how each metric is constructed, where the numbers come from, and when a metric can mislead you.
